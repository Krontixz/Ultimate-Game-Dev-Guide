# 🛠️ Quest 00: The Forge Setup
### *Preparing your Blacksmith Shop*

Before we can write a single line of code, we need to set up the **The Forge**—the environment where your code is written, tested, and turned into a game.

---

## 📜 The 5-Year-Old Explanation
Imagine you want to paint a picture. You need a canvas, brushes, and paint. 
* **VS Code** is your canvas and brushes (where you draw).
* **Node.js** and **.NET** are your paint (the stuff that makes the colors appear).
* **GitHub** is your art gallery (where you show the world what you made).

---

## 🔧 1. The Workbench: Visual Studio Code
VS Code is the world's most popular code editor. It is lightweight and works for both JS and C#.

1.  **Download:** [code.visualstudio.com](https://code.visualstudio.com/)
2.  **The Extensions (Essential):** Open VS Code, click the Extensions icon (4 squares), and install these:
    * **C# Dev Kit:** Official Microsoft support for C#.
    * **ESLint:** Warns you when your JavaScript is "smelly" (has errors).
    * **Prettier:** Automatically fixes your messy spacing.

---

## 🧪 2. The Alchemy (Runtimes)
You need to install the "engines" that allow your computer to understand your code.

### **JavaScript (Node.js)**
1.  Go to [nodejs.org](https://nodejs.org/).
2.  Download the **LTS (Long Term Support)** version.
3.  **Check it:** Open your terminal (Command Prompt) and type `node -v`. If a number appears, you win!

### **C# (.NET SDK)**
1.  Go to [dotnet.microsoft.com](https://dotnet.microsoft.com/download).
2.  Download the **.NET SDK**.
3.  **Check it:** In your terminal, type `dotnet --version`.

---

## 📖 3. Organization Strategy
In your GitHub, we will avoid generic names. Every lesson will have its own descriptive file.
* **Bad:** `Quest-01/readme.md`
* **Good:** `Quest-01/first-steps.md`

Each folder will also contain a `/code` folder where you save your `.js` and `.cs` scripts.

## 💎 Pro-Tip: The Terminal
You will see the word **Terminal** or **Console** a lot. This is just a text-box where you talk directly to your computer. In VS Code, you can open it by pressing `Ctrl + ` ` (the backtick key).
